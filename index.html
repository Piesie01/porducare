OUR SOCIAL INTERVENTIONS

How we add our Quota in making the World
lively for the less previlaged

We Help and Engage Women in Rural Areas in Raw Shea Butter Production.

As part of our efforts to help the aged make a decend living, we them to produce raw shea butter since the shea seeds are readily available in their communities and farms. We buy the shea butter for further distribution or processing into creams and body lotions. We also link them to other businesses that use shea butter as raw materials as a means of helping them reach a larger market.
This enables them to earn a descent living as well as reduce their dependency on other people (Their children and other relatives).



We Train and Mentor Young Ladies and in Rural Areas to Acquire Skills that can help them make a Decent living.

With the increasing cases of men taking advantage of young ladies in rural areas due to thier (the ladies') inability to provide basic needs for themselves, Porducare Enterprise has decided to help curb the situation by organizing skills training workshops for women and young girls in Northern Rural Areas. Among other skills taught are liquid soap making and how to add value to shea butter for sale.
This is also geared towards making these young ladies and women economically engaged as well as financially independent. This prevents them from engaging in social vices that may be caused by idleness.



We help Donate Sanitory Pads to Girls in School.

The galloping increase in price of sanitory pads in recent months has made it difficult for young girls at school going age to afford them. In light of this, Porducare Enterprise works hand in hand with benevolent individuals in the society to help donate sanitary pads to young girls in some rural communities in Northern Ghana. Most of these ladies tend to drop out of school in an attempt to escape stigmatization from their peers. The cost of these sanitory pads also makes these young girls vulnerable as men tend to take advantage on them before helping them purchase these sanitory pads. This regular intervention in fueld by our desire to empower women as well as help young girls in rural areas live decent lives.



We Donate Footware and Learning Materials to Learners in Rural communities.

One of the main challenges teachers in rural communities face is lack teaching and learning resources. Most of learners in these communities go to school without books and writing materials or if lucky, with one book for all the subjects.
We regularly donate footware and learning materials such as pencils and exercise books; through the support of individuals and other organizations to help keep these children from financially challeged homes in school. We do this to motivate and inspire these learners to attend school regularly. Since most families in the rural areas struggle to provide three-square meal daily, their challenge in providing books and other learning materials for their children is considerably understandable; hence the intervention.



Regular Counseling and Empowerment of Girls to remain in school.

At regular intervals, we organize girl-child empowerment sessions for girls in school. Since most girls within the adolescent ages are inquisitive to try what others do for personal experiences, it is eminent we guide them from peer inflence. It also prudent that these young girls who have decided against the status quo in rural communities to stay in school be given regular encouragement and assistance on their journey to being responsible adults.
This is to motivate and inspire them to aspire to be great women and women of value in the society. Among other topics that are discussed include career paths, responsible parenting, the role of women in nation building among other topics which will arm the young girls to aspire to be responsible adults in future.



Paching Torn Uniforms of Learners.

Teaching and learning becomes fun and effective if all learners are in decent attires or uniforms. It makes learners look smart and presentable as well as prevent stigmatization from the few who are descent. Although that is their parents' reponsibilty, these parents or relatives can not be blammed entirely since a three-square meal daily is a challenge in these communities.
To help bridge the gab, we also engage in a once a while patching of uniforms exercise for learners in rural communities in the Sawla Tuna Kalba district. This is done to ensure that learners appear neat and presentable in school to study. This activity is mostly carried out together with some liked minded entreprenuers who also have vested interest in helping learners in rural areas.



We Protect and Plant Trees

Due to human activities such as mining, farming, sand wining, burning of charcoal, among others, millions of trees have been cut down to pave way for the afforementioned activities. This has resulted in global warming and climate change in most parts of the world including Ghana. It is also worth noting that, women in the northern Ghana cut down shea trees to burn charchoal, sell in order to support their families. This is a desturbing situation but efforts have been made to curb the situation.
As we train these women to acquire profitable skills, we also admonish them to protect their business by protecting the shea trees. In addition to that, engage in tree plantation.



We Admonish our Customers and Trainees to Work Safely and with Happiness

Most of the women we engage are elderly women and young girls so we prioritize their well-being and happiness. We pay regular visit to our customers and mentees to make sure they work under safe conditions and to also make sure they are free from work related hazards. Through our regular visits, encouragement and support for them, they are able to work in happiness despite the challenges they may have at home or with family.
This we do to sustain and improve their physical and mental well-being as they engage in their daily economic activities.

About Us

We Educate, Empower and Inspire.

Porducare Enterprise is a social enterprise that is dedicated to the production and branding of quality shea butter to provide a ready market for local shea butter producers, create employment opportunities for the youth, empower women economically, and protect the shea trees. The shea industry is a major source of livelihood for over two million households in Northern Ghana and has the potential of alleviating poverty and creating sustainable jobs. Despite the huge market size, local shea producers do not get their rightful share as a result of low quality and poor packaging. In effect, local shea producers in northern Ghana fell the Shea trees and burn them into charcoal. Others move to urban cities to seek non-existing greener pastures.
